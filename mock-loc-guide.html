Mock Loc — your location testing buddy

Mock Loc is an Android app built for researchers, QA engineers, and developers who need precise control over GPS. Fake where you are — hold still, hop between pins, or travel along a realistic route — while other apps read your mocked coordinates.

What Mock Loc does

Tagline: Your location testing buddy. Mock Loc lets you choose how apps see your location — not where you physically are. Pin a spot on the map, queue multiple stops, or simulate movement along a drawn route with custom speed and GPS accuracy.

Whether you are validating a delivery app in another city, testing geo-fenced features, or running market-research scenarios in the field, Mock Loc gives you a polished map-first workflow instead of fiddling with developer options alone.

The home screen organizes everything around one idea: Fake your GPS. Pick how apps should see your location, then dive into the mode that matches your test.

Three core mocking modes

Mock Loc’s power comes from three complementary modules. Each one solves a different testing pattern — from standing still to cycling pins to moving along a path.

📍

Stationary pointer

Hold one fixed GPS pin. Apps see you at a single spot with tunable accuracy.

🔁

Stationary playlist

Cycle through saved pins automatically — each stop gets its own time and accuracy.

🛣️

Automatic itinerary

Draw routes on the map and simulate realistic travel at the speed you choose.

Stationary pointer — pin, tune, replay

The stationary pointer is Mock Loc’s foundation. Drop a pin anywhere on the map, save it to your history, adjust GPS accuracy, mark favourites, and replay the same spot whenever you need it.

What you can do

  • Place a pin on the map and mock your device GPS at that exact coordinate.
  • Save locations with names and descriptions for quick reuse.
  • Tune accuracy so the mocked fix looks tight (precise) or loose (realistic urban GPS).
  • Favourite important pins and browse your stationary history tray.
  • Play, pause, and stop mocking from the in-app player or notification shade.
Pin point Accuracy History Favourites

When mocking runs, Mock Loc keeps a foreground service alive so coordinates stay stable. Your actual phone location is stored separately in Settings → Map defaults — place search and nearby results still use your real position while you mock somewhere else.

Stationary playlist — multi-pin automation

Need to simulate visiting several locations in sequence without manual taps? Build a stationary playlist: a ordered queue of saved pins that Mock Loc hops through automatically.

Playlist highlights

  • Create pin queues with per-point dwell time and accuracy settings.
  • Preview all pins on the map before you hit play.
  • Enable loop mode to repeat the entire sequence.
  • Switch tray views — grid, comfy, list — to match how you browse playlists.
  • Edit playlists: add, remove, reorder points, rename, and share with teammates.

Automatic itinerary — moving routes

When your test needs movement — not just fixed pins — use Automatic itinerary. Draw route segments on the map, set travel speed and accuracy per segment, and simulate a trip that other apps believe you are taking.

Route building

  • Create named itineraries and add multiple route segments.
  • Draw paths on the map or enter latitude/longitude manually.
  • Compute driving or walking routes via directions APIs, with fallback to direct lines.
  • Customize each segment: speed (0–100 km/h), accuracy (0–100 m), and route colour.
  • Highlight individual segments, edit while stopped, and favourite key routes.

Playback experience

During playback, the notification shows progress — current segment, start, destination, and live speed/accuracy. The player displays percentage progress so you always know where you are along the route. Stop the itinerary before editing segments; Mock Loc protects running sessions from accidental changes.

Notification controls — mock without reopening the app

Mock Loc is designed for long-running tests. While stationary, playlist, or itinerary playback runs in the background, a persistent notification gives you full transport controls: play, pause, stop, previous, next, and favourite — without switching back to the app.

Account, cloud sync & data safety

Sign in to sync your stationary points, playlists, and itineraries across devices. Mock Loc supports email/phone login, Google sign-in, and account creation with optional developer profile fields.

What syncs when you log in

  • Stationary points — saved pins and history.
  • Stationary playlists — pin queues and their settings.
  • Itineraries — multi-segment route plans.

After login, a step-by-step sync screen collects each module so you can see progress in real time. Keep the app open — it usually finishes in a few seconds.

Background auto sync

In Settings → Developers → General, enable Auto sync in background. Mock Loc uploads unsaved locations and playlists even when the app is closed. Choose your check interval:

  • Every 30 minutes, 1 hour, 4 hours (recommended default), 6 hours, 12 hours, or 24 hours.
  • When logged out but data is pending, you get a reminder notification to sign in.
  • Android may batch checks slightly to save battery — notifications must be allowed.

Map defaults worth knowing

Mock Loc stores your phone’s real GPS separately from the mocked position. Place search and Google APIs use your actual coordinates for nearby results while you mock a different spot on the map. Refresh it in Settings when mocking is off for an accurate baseline.

For developers — diagnostics & integrations

Mock Loc includes a password-protected Developers tab for power users building on top of the Research-Work API ecosystem.

Developer corner
  • API environment picker — override the base URL from Firebase Remote Config (debug builds can point at a local Research-Work-API server).
  • Admin notifications — opt in to push usage events to the admin dashboard for monitoring field activity.
  • OneSignal diagnostics — inspect external ID sync, audience tags, and re-sync when push targeting drifts.
  • Analytics events — the app emits structured ml_* Firebase events (playback, edits, sync, auth) visible in the adminweb usage report.
  • Mock coins — session-based usage metering for play actions; developers on unlimited plans can mock freely.

Debug builds expose extra tooling: test crash reporting to Crashlytics, API base URL overrides via local.properties, and verbose sync scheduling feedback. Release builds use Firebase Remote Config for production API endpoints.

Bottom line

Mock Loc turns Android GPS mocking into a visual, repeatable workflow. Start with a stationary pin, graduate to playlists for multi-stop tests, and use itineraries when movement matters. Sign in to keep everything backed up, enable background sync for peace of mind, and control it all from your notification shade while you test other apps.
