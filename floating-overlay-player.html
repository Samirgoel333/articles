What the floating overlay player is

It is a compact system overlay card that appears only while Mock Loc is in the background and a mock session is active. Inside the app you still use the normal in-app player. When you switch away, the floating card takes over so you can keep testing without friction.

Good to know: the overlay never replaces mocking itself. If you skip the display-over-apps permission, or hide the card with ✕, your mock session and sticky notification keep working exactly as before.

How to turn the floating player on

Android needs Display over other apps (also called Appear on top) permission before Mock Loc can draw the card. Mock Loc asks gently after you start a mock — and you can always skip.

1
Start any mock as usual

Open Stationary Pointer, Playlist, or Auto Itinerary and tap Play (or Mock). The in-app player appears while you stay inside Mock Loc.

2
Allow “display over other apps” when prompted

If permission is missing, Mock Loc shows a short explain dialog. Tap Allow and enable Mock Loc in Android settings. You can Skip anytime — mocking is never blocked. Only check Don’t ask me again if you truly want to stop seeing the prompt.

3
Leave Mock Loc — the card appears

Press Home or switch to another app. While mocking is still running, the floating player appears over that app. Drag it from the left handle if you want it out of the way; Mock Loc remembers the position.

Turn permission on later from Android

  • Open Android Settings → Apps → Mock Loc → Appear on top / Display over other apps.
  • Enable the toggle, then leave Mock Loc again while a mock is running.
  • If you previously chose “Don’t ask me again,” use Settings to re-enable the overlay yourself.

Using the player controls

Each module gets a card tailored to that workflow. Controls mirror the sticky notification and in-app player so you never learn a second language.

What you can do from the card

  • Play / Pause / Stop — same actions as the notification.
  • Favourite — stationary and playlist points.
  • Previous / Next — playlist pins only.
  • Tap the title area — opens the matching module inside Mock Loc.
  • Drag the handle — move the card; position is saved.

How to hide or turn the floating player off

You stay in charge. Hide the card for a moment, or leave the Android permission off if you prefer notification-only control.

1
Hide for now — tap the red ✕

The close badge sits on the top-right corner of the card. Tap it to dismiss the overlay only. Mocking keeps running. Open Mock Loc (or any app that brings Mock Loc to the foreground), then leave again — the player reappears while the session is still active.

2
Return to Mock Loc — card auto-hides

Opening Mock Loc (launcher icon, notification, or tapping the card title) dismisses the overlay automatically. Use the normal in-app player while you are inside the app.

3
Fully disable — turn off Appear on top

In Android Settings, disable Mock Loc’s Appear on top permission. Overlay cards will no longer show. Sticky notifications and in-app players still control mocking. Start a new mock later and allow the permission again if you want the floating player back.

Quick tips

Get the most from the overlay

  • Drag once, reuse forever — place the card where your thumb likes it; Mock Loc remembers.
  • ✕ ≠ Stop — close only hides the UI; use Stop when you want mocking to end.
  • Permission is optional — Skip never pauses your mock session.
  • One module at a time — stop the current mock before starting another module’s session.

Troubleshooting

I never see the floating player

Confirm a mock is actually running, then fully leave Mock Loc (Home or recent apps). Check that Appear on top is enabled for Mock Loc. Some OEMs bury this under special app access — search for “appear on top” or “display over other apps.”

I dismissed the card and it did not come back

After you tap ✕, open Mock Loc once (or bring it to the foreground), then switch away again. The overlay is designed to return on the next background trip while mocking is still active.

The red close button opens the module instead

Make sure you are on the latest Mock Loc build. The close badge is meant to sit above the card and only dismiss the overlay. Prefer tapping the center of the red circle.
