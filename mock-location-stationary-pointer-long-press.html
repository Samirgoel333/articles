Stationary Pointer guide

Mock any location with a long press

Stationary Pointer is the fastest way to place your GPS at one fixed spot. Long press the map where you want to be, confirm the coordinates, and Mock Loc starts reporting that position to every app on your phone — until you press Stop.

What Stationary Pointer does

Unlike a route or playlist, Stationary Pointer does not move through multiple places. It holds your mock GPS at the exact coordinate you choose until you stop it. Think of it as standing still at one address while other apps believe you are really there.

📌

Best for one-place testing

Test check-ins, local search, delivery zones, dating apps, games, or any workflow that depends on a single fixed GPS position.

⚡

No typing required

Long press is ideal when you can see the location on the map and want to start mocking without opening search or entering latitude and longitude.

💾

Saved to history

When the point is new, Mock Loc saves it in your stationary history so you can mock the same place again later with one tap.

When to use long press vs other methods: Use long press when you already know roughly where on the map you want to be. Use Search when you know a place name or address. Use Manual when you have exact coordinates. Use Nearby to browse places around your real location.

Before you begin

Stationary Pointer works best when a few basics are already in place:

Checklist

  • Developer options enabled and Mock Loc selected as your mock location app. See the setup guide →
  • Location permission granted to Mock Loc when prompted.
  • No other mock running — stop any active route, playlist, or stationary session first.

Long press only works when no stationary player is already running. If you see active playback controls or a pulsing pin, stop the current mock before selecting a new point.

How to start a stationary mock — step by step

These four steps match the current Mock Loc flow: long press selects the point, a bottom sheet confirms it, and the Mock button starts playback.

1
Open Stationary Pointer

From the Mock Loc home screen, open the Stationary Pointer module. You will see the map, your saved location tray at the bottom, and quick actions such as Search, My location, Manual, and Nearby.

2
Long press anywhere on the map

Touch and hold the exact spot you want to mock for about one second. The app drops a marker at that coordinate and moves the camera to centre on it. A subtle touch ring may appear while your finger is down — that is normal.

3
Review the confirmation sheet

Mock Loc opens a bottom sheet titled something like Mock Selected Point. It shows the point name (or coordinates) so you can verify the location before anything starts. If you tapped the wrong place, press Cancel — the map and history tray return to their previous state.

4
Tap Mock to start

When you are happy with the selection, tap Mock. Mock Loc saves the new point to your history if it is not already saved, then starts stationary location playback. The marker switches to a pulsing active state and player controls appear so you can stop whenever you are done.

What you will see on screen

  • A bottom sheet with coordinates (for example, 28.6329, 77.2812).
  • Cancel and Mock buttons — only Mock starts the session.
  • After Mock: a pulsing pin, hidden history tray, and a player bar with Stop.

What happens after you tap Mock

The point becomes active immediately. Mock Loc starts its stationary point service for that saved location and keeps it running in the foreground so coordinates stay stable while you switch to other apps.

While mocking is running

  • The map marker pulses to show the active mock.
  • The history tray hides so the map stays clear.
  • Player controls appear — use them or the notification shade to Stop.
  • A persistent notification keeps the mock alive in the background.
  • Other apps on your phone read the mocked coordinates, not your physical location.

When you are finished testing, press Stop in the player or notification. Your phone returns to reporting real GPS (unless you start another mock session).

Quick tips before you long press

A few small checks help the mock start cleanly and make the selected point easier to reuse later.

Pro tips

  • Use long press for speed — when you can see the place on the map and do not need to search by name.
  • Use Search for named places — businesses, landmarks, and full addresses are faster to find by text.
  • Avoid your exact current location — Android may not need to simulate anything if you are already physically there; pick a clearly different spot on the map.
  • Stop other mocks first — routes, playlists, and stationary sessions cannot run at the same time.
  • Zoom in for precision — pinch to zoom before long pressing so your finger lands on the exact building or intersection you need.
  • Rename saved points — after saving, give the point a clear name in history so you can find it again quickly.

Other ways to pick a point

Long press is the fastest method, but Stationary Pointer offers more options when you need them:

Troubleshooting

Long press does nothing

Make sure no stationary mock is already running — stop it first. Also confirm you are pressing on the map itself, not on a button or the history tray.

Bottom sheet does not appear

Hold your finger slightly longer (about one full second). If the map was still moving from a previous gesture, wait for it to settle and try again.

Other apps still show my real location

Verify Mock Loc is selected under Developer options → Select mock location app. Confirm you tapped Mock (not just Cancel) and that the player shows an active session. Some apps cache location — force-close them and reopen after starting the mock.

I picked the wrong spot

Press Stop, then long press the correct location. You can also drag the map and zoom in for better accuracy before your next attempt.
