How Mock Loc syncs and backs up your data

Your saved locations, playlists, and routes belong to you. This page explains exactly when Mock Loc uploads data to the server, what you will see on screen, and why — so you never have to guess what the app is doing behind the scenes.

Why we publish this guide

Our promise: Mock Loc handles location testing data that matters to your work. We believe you should know when data leaves your phone, what is included, and how to control it — without reading technical docs or reverse-engineering the app.

Sync is not a hidden background process. The app tells you when changes are waiting to back up, shows a banner on the home screen, and posts notifications during upload. If you are logged out, it reminds you to sign in instead of silently failing. This guide mirrors the real behaviour in the current Mock Loc Android app.

What gets backed up

Mock Loc syncs three types of data tied to your account. Everything else stays on your device unless you explicitly share it (for example, sharing a playlist link).

📍 Stationary points

Saved pins — names, coordinates, accuracy, favourites, icons, and history entries you created on the map.

🔁 Stationary playlists

Named pin queues, loop settings, colours, and each stop inside a playlist with its timing and accuracy.

🛣️ Automatic itineraries

Route plans and their segments — drawn paths, speeds, accuracy per segment, and travel mode (walk, drive, direct).

Upload order

When a sync runs, Mock Loc uploads in a fixed sequence so the server always receives a consistent snapshot:

  1. Stationary points first
  2. Playlists (including their points) second
  3. Itineraries (routes and segments) last

After each step succeeds, the app marks that data as synced on your phone. If a step fails, you will see an error and can tap Sync to try again.

When sync happens

Sync is triggered in four main situations. Each one is designed for a different moment in your workflow.

👆

You tap Sync on the home screen

Manual backup. Opens login if you are signed out; otherwise uploads all pending changes immediately. Shows progress in the banner and notification shade.

🔑

You log in or sign up

Mock Loc downloads your cloud data first (points → playlists → itineraries), then uploads anything you created as a guest on this device.

🔄

Background auto sync (scheduled)

If enabled in Settings → Developers → General, Mock Loc checks on a timer (default every 4 hours) whether anything needs uploading — even when the app is closed.

✏️

You save data or leave the app

After you create or edit a point, playlist, or itinerary, Mock Loc queues a near-term background check. Leaving the app also triggers a check if auto sync is on.

Manual sync — step by step

This is what happens when you tap Sync on the home screen while logged in.

Manual sync flow

You tap Sync Home screen banner or sync button
Are you logged in? If not → opens the login screen
Count pending changes Banner lists locations, playlists, and routes waiting to back up
Progress notification appears “Syncing Mock Loc data” in your notification shade
Upload stationary points Then playlists, then itineraries — one after another
Done — banner hides Badge shows “Synced”. Notification is dismissed.

While sync runs, the banner shows a progress bar and the message Syncing…. You can leave the app — upload continues in the background via a foreground service so Android does not stop it mid-way.

Login sync — download then upload

Signing in is a two-way process. Mock Loc first pulls your latest cloud backup onto the phone, then pushes any local-only changes you made before logging in.

Login / sign-up sync flow

Account verified Email, phone, or Google sign-in succeeds
Download stationary points Progress screen: “Collecting your stationary points…”
Download playlists Step 2 on the sync progress panel
Download itineraries Step 3 — routes and segments from your account
Guest data on this device? If you used the app before logging in, local changes are merged
Upload merged changes Background upload starts; you land on the home screen

Keep the app open during the download steps — it usually finishes in a few seconds. The footer hint on the login sync screen says: “This usually takes a few seconds. Please keep the app open.”

Background auto sync — what happens while you are away

Auto sync is on by default (you can turn it off in Settings → Developers → General). When enabled, Android schedules periodic checks using your chosen interval.

Background check decision tree

Timer fires Default: every 4 hours · also after you save data or close the app
Any unsaved changes? If nothing pending → check ends quietly, no notification
Logged out High-priority reminder: “Unsaved Mock Loc data — log in and tap Sync”
Logged in Upload starts with a low-priority progress notification
Network required No internet → sync retries later; nothing is lost on your phone

Interval options

You can choose how often background checks run:

30 min 1 hour 4 hours (default) 6 hours 12 hours 24 hours

Shorter intervals keep backups fresher but use slightly more battery. Android may batch checks to save power — that is normal system behaviour, not Mock Loc ignoring your setting.

The home screen sync banner

The peach-coloured bar below your greeting is the sync banner. It is your at-a-glance backup status — not an ad, not a generic alert.

✅ Banner appears when

  • You have unsaved changes on this device (new or edited points, playlists, or routes).
  • You are on the home screen and not actively mocking a location.
  • The banner shows how many items are waiting and a View details link listing each one.
  • If logged out, the greeting area shows Login to Sync Data instead of a sync badge.

🚫 Banner is hidden when

  • Everything is synced — badge reads “Synced” and the banner slides away.
  • You are actively mocking (stationary, playlist, or itinerary player is running on the home screen).
  • A sync is not needed because there are zero pending changes.

Banner states while you interact

State What you see
Idle — pending data “You have updates not backed up yet” + Sync button + optional View details
Syncing Progress bar, Syncing… label, step text (points → playlists → itineraries)
Failed “Backup could not finish. Tap Sync now to try again.” + error snackbar
All clear Banner hidden · badge shows Synced

Notifications you may see

Mock Loc uses two notification channels so you can tell progress apart from reminders. Allow notifications for the best experience — especially if you use auto sync while logged out.

Data sync

Syncing Mock Loc data

Shown during upload. Title: Syncing Mock Loc data. Body counts pending items and the current step. Tap to open the app. Dismissed when sync completes.

Sync reminders

Unsaved Mock Loc data

Shown when auto sync finds pending data but you are not logged in. High priority — taps open the login screen. Cancelled once you sign in.

Account restore

Updating your Mock Loc data

Rare — used when an admin-triggered restore downloads the latest server copy to your device. Separate from everyday backup uploads.

Controlling auto sync

Go to Settings → Developers → General → Background sync (Developer options tab must be unlocked). Here you can:

  • Toggle Auto sync in background on or off.
  • Pick your check interval (30 minutes to 24 hours).
  • Read the built-in note on how reminders work when logged out.

When you change the interval, Mock Loc shows a confirmation snackbar: “Background sync scheduled — checks run every [interval].” A near-term check is queued so you can verify notifications within about 10 seconds.

What we do not sync

Stays on your device

  • Live GPS mocking sessions — only your saved data syncs, not real-time location streams.
  • Your physical phone location — stored separately for map search; not uploaded as mock data.
  • App settings — map theme, developer toggles, and UI preferences stay local unless noted.
  • Support tickets / queries — handled through a separate help flow, not the backup sync.

Sync requires a network connection and a valid login. Data is sent over HTTPS to the Mock Loc API servers associated with your account — the same endpoints the app uses for /sync-stationary-points, /sync-playlists, and /sync-itineraries.

Quick FAQ

Can I use Mock Loc without an account?

Yes. You can save points, playlists, and routes locally. They stay on your phone until you log in and sync. The banner will prompt you when backup is needed.

Will sync interrupt my mock session?

The sync banner hides while you are actively mocking on the home screen so it does not get in the way. Background upload can still run if you started sync before mocking, or via auto sync while you use other apps.

Why did auto sync not run exactly on time?

Android optimises background work for battery life. Checks may arrive a few minutes late, especially on aggressive battery savers. Your data remains safe on the device until the next successful upload.

What if sync fails?

Your changes are not deleted. The banner shows a failure message. Tap Sync again when you have a stable connection. Check that you are still logged in.
