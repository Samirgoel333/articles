How to start a stationary mock

These steps match the Mock Loc flow: long press selects the point, the bottom sheet confirms it, and the Mock button starts playback.

1
Open Stationary Pointer

From Mock Loc, open the Stationary Pointer module. You will see the map, the saved location tray, and quick actions like Search, My location, Manual, and Nearby.

2
Long press on the map

Touch and hold the exact place you want to mock. The app drops a marker at that coordinate and moves the camera to it. Long press works only when no stationary player is already running.

3
Review the confirmation sheet

Mock Loc shows a bottom sheet with the selected point name and coordinates, so you can confirm before the mock starts. If you cancel, the map and history tray return to the previous state.

4
Tap Mock to start

When you tap Mock, Mock Loc saves the new point to your history if needed and starts stationary location playback. The marker changes to a pulsing active state and player controls appear.

One selected point, held steady

Unlike a route or playlist, Stationary Pointer does not move through many places. It keeps your mock GPS at the exact point you choose until you press Stop.

📌

Best for one-place testing

Test check-ins, local search results, delivery areas, dating apps, games, or any workflow that depends on one fixed GPS position.

⚡

No typing required

Long press is useful when you can see the location on the map and want to start mocking without opening search or entering coordinates.

💾

Reusable from history

When the point is new, Mock Loc saves it in your stationary history so you can mock the same place again later.

What happens after you tap Mock

The point becomes active immediately. After you tap Mock, the app starts the stationary point service for that saved point. The marker pulses on the map, the history tray hides, and the player controls become available so you can stop the mock whenever you are done.

While mocking is running

  • The map marker pulses to show the active mock.
  • Player controls and a notification let you stop from anywhere.
  • Other apps read the mocked coordinates, not your physical location.

Quick tips before you long press

Pro tips

  • Use a long press for a quick point when you already know the place visually on the map.
  • Use Search if you want to find a business, address, or landmark by name.
  • Do not pick your exact current location because Android may not need to simulate anything there.
  • Stop any running route, playlist, or stationary mock before starting a new one.
